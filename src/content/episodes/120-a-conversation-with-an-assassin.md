### 1. The Hypocrites and the Martyrs of Al-Raji’
Following the betrayal at Al-Raji’, where six Sahaba were killed or executed, the **hypocrites (munafiqoon)** in Madinah mocked the victims, calling them foolish for dying far from their families. In response, Allah revealed verses in **Surat Al-Baqarah (2:204–207)**. These verses contrast the "impressive" and smooth speech of the hypocrites—who cause corruption while claiming to be good—with the nobility of the believers who "sell" their lives purely to seek the **approval of Allah**.

### 2. The Assassination Plot
Abu Sufyan, frustrated by his inability to defeat the Prophet (saw) on the battlefield, attempted a **covert assassination**. He pointed out to the Quraysh that the Prophet was not a heavily guarded figure and walked the streets of Madinah freely. A Bedouin expert tracker and guide accepted the job, boasting of his ability to travel stealthily and his skill with a **hidden dagger**. Abu Sufyan provided him with money and a camel, emphasizing that the mission must remain a secret to avoid alerting the Muslims.

### 3. Divine Insight and Prophetic Mercy
The assassin reached Madinah in six days and found the Prophet (saw) with the **Banu Abdul Ash-hal** tribe. As the man entered the gathering, the Prophet (saw) immediately warned his companions: **"This man is not here with good intentions"**. Before the assassin could strike, **Usayd ibn Hudayr**—a pillar of the Madinan community—grabbed him and discovered the concealed dagger. While Usayd was prepared to use force, the Prophet (saw) intervened and offered the man **immunity and protection** if he told the truth.

### 4. The Assassin’s Conversion
After confessing the details of Abu Sufyan’s plot, the assassin was kept safely overnight. The next day, the Prophet (saw) offered him freedom or the "better option" of Islam. The man accepted Islam immediately, testifying that he was a "ruthless" individual who feared no one, yet he began to **tremble** the moment he laid eyes on the Prophet. He realized the Prophet (saw) possessed **divine protection** because he had spotted him instantly despite the man's expertise in being "invisible" and stealthy.

### 5. The Mission to Makkah: Amr ibn Umayyah
In response to Abu Sufyan’s persistent attempts at assassination and the execution of Khubayb, the Prophet (saw) sent **Amr ibn Umayyah ad-Damri** and **Salama ibn Aslam** to Makkah to see if they could take out Abu Sufyan. Amr was a famous "roughneck" from the days of *jahiliyyah* and was highly recognizable. Despite his warnings, Salama insisted they enter the Haram to pray, where they were spotted and recognized by **Muawiyah ibn Abu Sufyan**, putting all of Makkah on "red alert".

### 6. Escape and the Recovery of Khubayb
The two Sahaba fled to the mountains and hid in a crevice for two days while Makkan search parties scoured the area. During their return journey, Salama suggested they **dignify their martyred brother, Khubayb**, whose body was still tied to a pole where he had been executed. In a daring night operation, Amr carried Khubayb’s body over his shoulder. When a guard woke up and gave chase, Amr had to flee, but he successfully managed to **kick some dirt over the body** to provide at least a symbolic burial before escaping.

### 7. The Relentless Hostility of the Quraysh
Amr faced several more trials on his way back to Madinah, including a confrontation with a hostile man and an encounter with **bounty hunters**. He eventually returned to Madinah, where the Prophet (saw) greeted him with a smile and made *dua* for him. This entire series of events—from the hired assassin to the pursuit of Amr—served as a stark reminder that the people of Makkah were **unwilling to "bury the hatchet"** and would continue to seek any opportunity to destroy the Muslim community.
